OUR COURSES


ADCA

(Advance Diploma in Computer Application)

This course is a one-year program that enhances IT and computer application skills. It's designed to prepare students for careers in the IT field by providing in-depth knowledge of computer applications and related IT skills. The course curriculum covers a wide range of topics, including software programming, database management, and system administration.





DCA

(Diploma in Computer Application)

This is a short-term, vocational program that provides foundational knowledge of computer applications and IT. It's typically a 6 to 12-month course designed to enhance computer literacy and technical skills, preparing individuals for entry-level roles or further education in computer science.





TALLY

(Transactions Allowed in a Linear Line Yard)

This course teaches individuals how to use Tally, a widely used accounting and ERP software, to manage financial records, inventory, and payroll. It's a job-oriented course that's popular among students pursuing careers in accounting and finance.





DTP

(Desktop Publishing)

This computer course teaches you how to create professional-looking printed and digital documents using computer software. It covers design principles, layout, typography, and software skills for tools like Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, and Photoshop. The course prepares you for jobs in publishing, graphic design, and related fields.





INTERNET

(Interconnected Network)

This course can refer to a variety of learning options, from introductory level to advanced, focusing on the internet, networking, and computer technology. These courses cover topics like computer networking fundamentals, the internet protocol (TCP/IP), cybersecurity, and the Internet of Things (IoT).





MS-OFFICE

(Microsoft Office)

This course teaches you how to use the core applications in Microsoft Office, such as Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook, for tasks like document creation, data analysis, presentations, and email management. These skills are essential in many workplaces and can enhance your productivity and communication.





TYPING

Typing, in simple terms, is the act of writing using a keyboard to produce text on a computer or other device. It involves pressing keys on a keyboard to input letters, numbers, and symbols. The process of typing can be learned and practiced to improve speed and accuracy, with many online tools and resources available to aid in this process.
